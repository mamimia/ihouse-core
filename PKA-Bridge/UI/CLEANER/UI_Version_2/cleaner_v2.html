Design Notes
Role Personality
Photo-first, room-by-room. Cleaner work is spatial (one room at a time) and evidence-based (photos prove readiness). Task cards show a photo badge with required count. Progress is tracked in room dots, not step numbers. The vocabulary is clean: "Clean", "Done", "Ready" — never "Task" or "Step".
Urgency System
Window-based countdown — cleaners work within a check-in window (e.g., 12:00–16:00), not a precise departure time. Countdown shows time until window closes. Safe, Approaching, Imminent, and Overdue states drive visual hierarchy with specific color and animation treatments.
Distinct from Check-In/Out
Cleaner cards have room dots (spatial progress), a photo count badge (evidence requirement), and a window label instead of guest name. The active screen shows a room list, not a condition checklist. Completion is "Property Ready" — a satisfying visual payoff.
What Stayed Calm
Room detail screen is calm and focused. Photo slots are clear and inviting, not demanding. The Property Ready screen is the most calm surface: large green check, confirmation summary, no urgency. Moss green (#334036) signals readiness and completion.
