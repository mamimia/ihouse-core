CHECK-IN & CHECK-OUT — Operational UI Direction

Clean foundation · Urgency layer added · 4 screens

A · What was added
Day Health strip — live count of task types + overdue alert. Always visible, compact.

Navigation micro-icon — quiet 24×24 tap target on every card. No label. Tap → route to property.

4-state urgency system — Safe / Approaching / Imminent / Overdue. Each state changes border, background tint, countdown color, and CTA weight.
B · How urgency was added
Urgency is expressed through countdown pills, not clutter. One number — "in 9 min" — communicates more than three labels.

Background tint is very subtle (2–4% off-white), enough to read state at a glance without making cards feel heavy.

Imminent cards pulse gently — a soft box-shadow animation. The countdown blinks at 50% opacity. The nav icon shifts to copper.
C · Card scan order
A worker's eye moves: left border color (urgency) → property name → countdown pill (how long) → type badge (what to do) → CTA (do it) → nav icon (find it).

Six pieces of operational information. One glance. No search required.
D · What stayed calm
Background is still Stone Mist. White cards. Clean spacing.

Only items that deserve attention break through — overdue and imminent cards pierce the calm. Safe and approaching cards stay quiet.

History and Profile screens are unchanged — urgency signals only appear where operational pressure exists.
